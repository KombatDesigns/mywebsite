Wealth Management

Financial literacy is key to long-term security. Begin by building an emergency fund that covers 3–6 months of expenses. Use budgeting tools like YNAB or Mint to track spending. Contribute early to RRSPs and TFSAs to benefit from tax-sheltered growth. As income rises, consider asset diversification: ETFs, GICs, mutual funds, and later, real estate or private equity.

Insurance for Health Professionals

Protecting your income is non-negotiable. Start with long-term disability insurance early — the younger you are, the cheaper the premium. Supplement this with life and critical illness insurance. For physicians, consider OMA Insurance. For nurses and allied health, check with professional associations for group rates. Always read the fine print.

Pension Planning

HOOPP and OMERS offer defined benefit pensions with stable retirement income. Contributions are matched by your employer and benefits scale with years of service. Understand vesting periods and early withdrawal penalties. Not in a pension plan? Set up an Individual Pension Plan (IPP) or Personal Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) as your fallback.

Ontario Billing

Accurate billing with OHIP is essential for physicians. Use platforms like Dr. Bill or MDBilling.ca for real-time code lookup and claim tracking. Stay updated with Schedule of Benefits changes. Understand audit triggers and always document clearly. For new physicians, consider a billing agent until you're confident with self-billing.

Should You Incorporate?

Incorporating can reduce taxes through income splitting, retained earnings, and dividend strategies. However, it's only beneficial above certain income thresholds. Speak to an accountant to understand if it fits your situation. Consider professional corporations in Ontario and review CPA Canada's guidelines.

Student Loans & Debt Management

Repaying OSAP or bank loans? Focus on high-interest debt first. Consider refinancing for lower rates. Automate payments, avoid minimum-only strategies. As income stabilizes, use the 'snowball' or 'avalanche' methods. Don’t ignore loans post-residency — interest grows fast.
